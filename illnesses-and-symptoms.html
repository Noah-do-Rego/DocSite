Illnesses

Acne is a common skin condition that affects most people at some point. It causes spots, oily skin and sometimes skin that's hot or painful to touch. Acne most commonly develops on the face, back and chest.

Remedies:
Don't wash affected areas of skin more than twice a day. Frequent washing can irritate the skin and make symptoms worse
Wash the affected area with a mild soap or cleanser and lukewarm water. Very hot or cold water can make acne worse
Don't try to "clean out" blackheads or squeeze spots. This can make them worse and cause permanent scarring

An allergy is a reaction the body has to a particular food or substance (such as grass, chemicals, etc.). Symptoms of an allergy can include sneezing, a runny or blocked nose, watery eyes and more.

Remedies:
Try to determine what is causing your allergy and refrain from contact/ingestion of it
Contact your doctor for next steps to take

Asthma is a common long-term condition that can cause coughing, wheezing, chest tightness and breathlessness. It is caused by the inflamation of bronchi in your lungs.

Remedies:
Avoidance of possible triggers
The use of medication or an inhaler

Symptoms

A cough is a reflex action to clear your airways of mucus and irritants such as dust or smoke. Can be caused by asthma, allergies, inhaled dust or smoke, or more. If your cough is persistent or severe, consider calling your doctor.

Remedies:
Usage of cough medicine
Treatment of the underlying cause

A fever is a high temperature. Most fevers are caused by infections or other illnesses. The high body temperature makes it more difficult for the bacteria and viruses that cause infections to survive. Common conditions that cause fevers are ear infections, a flu, common childhood illnesses and more.

Remedies:
Stay hydrated by drinking lots of water
Usage of medicine
Treatment of the underlying cause

One of the most common health complaints. there are many different types of headaches, including tension headaches, migraines, cluster headaches, and more.

Remedies:
Try to determine what type of headache you have and its underlying cause
If the pain is severe, contact your doctor
